# S002 — Cone coverage vs. dialogical convergence in seeded deliberations

- **status:** pre-registered
- **type:** computational
- **registered-on:** 2026-05-27
- **author:** (programme seed)

## Hypothesis

**H1 (primary, directional).** In seeded ludics deliberations, the
*probability of convergence within K interaction steps* increases
monotonically with the *number of cones in `Inc(B)` visited by walked
witness records* (cone coverage), controlling for total walked-witness
count and design depth.

**H2 (secondary).** Cone coverage is a *better* predictor of convergence
than raw walked-witness count, in the sense that adding cone-coverage to a
walked-witness-only model significantly reduces residual deviance.

## Design

- **Data source.** Synthetic deliberations generated by the substrate's
  existing seed harnesses (cf.
  [`../../ludics-development-roadmaps-research/seededludicsdelibresults1.txt`](../../ludics-development-roadmaps-research/seededludicsdelibresults1.txt),
  `seededludicsdelibresults2.txt`, `seededludicsdelibresults3.txt`).
- **Sample size.** 200 deliberations, stratified to give a range of
  `|Inc(B)|` from 2 to 6.
- **Protocol.** Each deliberation runs to natural termination (daimon at
  some locus on either side) or to a fuel cap of K = 200 interaction
  steps.

## Metrics (frozen)

- **Primary outcome:** convergence within K steps (binary).
- **Predictor:** cone coverage = `|{Cᵢ : ∃ walked witness w ∈ Cᵢ}| /
  |Inc(B)|`.
- **Covariates:** walked-witness count, design depth (max locus path
  length), `|Inc(B)|`.
- **Subgroup:** by `|Inc(B)|` quartile.

## Analysis plan (frozen)

- Logistic regression: `converged ~ cone_coverage + walked_count +
  depth + |Inc(B)|`.
- **Supportive of H1:** cone-coverage coefficient β > 0 at α = 0.05.
- **Null:** β not significantly different from zero.
- **For H2:** likelihood-ratio test comparing the full model against
  `converged ~ walked_count + depth + |Inc(B)|`; supportive iff p < 0.05.

## Stopping rule

200 deliberations, no early stopping.
