The honest first observation is that the program as it stands rests on an *analogy* — claim is a Proponent design, challenge is an Opponent design, agreement is convergence, disagreement is divergence — and the entire research frontier opens once you try to turn that analogy into a theorem. Almost everything serious follows from taking the Ludics–argumentation correspondence and demanding that it be proved rather than asserted. Let me lay out where I think the real work goes, organized as six directions in rough order of foundational priority: (1) the foundational Dung↔Ludics bridge, (2) separation as the locus-of-disagreement theorem, (3) the quantitative core, (4) distributed and sheaf-theoretic semantics, (5) mechanization, and (6) the philosophy bridge. The first three are load-bearing for the platform's core claims; the last three are where the highest-ceiling and most exportable results live.

---

## 1. The foundational bridge: Dung acceptability ↔ Ludics interaction

The keystone result the whole program needs is a faithful correspondence between Dung-style argument acceptability and Ludics interaction. Right now the platform runs ASPIC+ underneath (defeasible rules, attack, defeat) and a Ludics interaction layer on top, and they're glued by intuition. The foundational theorem would be a soundness-and-completeness bridge: an argument is in the grounded (or preferred, or stable) extension of the abstract argumentation framework *if and only if* its corresponding design is orthogonal to every opponent design in the relevant behaviour. This is genuinely hard, and it's hard for a specific reason worth naming — Dung acceptability is a global fixpoint over an attack graph, a non-interactive property of the whole framework, whereas Ludics convergence is a local property of two designs interacting. The bridge already half-exists in the literature with: Modgil and Caminada's *argument games* give a dialogical, move-by-move characterization of grounded and preferred semantics, and Ludics is precisely a theory of dialogical interaction. So the tractable path is to show that the argument-game characterization of Dung semantics *is* the Ludics dispute, up to a translation that preserves winning strategies. If that goes through, the platform's core claim stops being marketing and becomes a corollary of proof theory. If it doesn't go through cleanly — and it might not, because the preferred semantics involves a maximality condition that has no obvious interactive counterpart — then the interesting result is the precise obstruction, the characterization of *which* argumentation semantics are interactively realizable and which are not. Either outcome is a real paper.

A full-abstraction result: a translation from argumentation structures (ASPIC+ arguments, or AIF graphs) into Ludics designs that is sound and complete with respect to acceptability — a claim is acceptable in the argumentation semantics if and only if its design is winning, i.e. orthogonal to the relevant counter-designs. The technical heart is the conjecture that the acceptable arguments for a claim form a Ludics behaviour — a set of designs closed under bi-orthogonality, B = B^⊥⊥. If you can show that Dung's grounded, preferred, and stable extensions correspond exactly to bi-orthogonally-closed sets of designs, you've shown that two theories of "what survives challenge," developed independently by Girard from proof theory and Dung from nonmonotonic reasoning, coincide — and, more valuably, you've characterized precisely where they diverge, because they almost certainly don't coincide everywhere, and the divergence is where the interesting structure lives. Girard's notion of internal completeness (a behaviour already contains every design you need without appeal to the ambient logic) becomes the formal version of a real design question: whether a deliberation is "closed" — whether every relevant argument is reachable from inside it — or whether it constitutively requires import from other rooms. That last point is not academic; it's the mathematical content of the Plexus.

## 2. Separation: the locus of disagreement as a theorem

The locus-of-disagreement capability — the "gap identification" the platform sells as its distinctive feature — should become a normalization and separation theorem rather than a heuristic. In Ludics, when two designs fail to be orthogonal, the dispute diverges at a determinate address: the first chronicle position where the two strategies stop matching. The theorem to pursue is that this divergence locus is unique, computable, and corresponds to a *minimal unshared commitment* — a presupposition one party holds and the other challenges or lacks. Girard's separation theorem (designs are distinguished by their behaviour under interaction, the Ludics analogue of Böhm's theorem) is the engine here. If you can show the divergence locus is the minimal separating context, then "the platform identifies exactly where you disagree" is not an engineering claim about a feature, it's a consequence of separation. Be precise about where the difficulty actually lives, though: uniqueness of the first-divergence address within a single dispute is plausibly easy, but the load-bearing claim is *minimality across all opponent designs* — that the locus is *the* minimal separating context, not merely *a* separating one — and that minimality argument, not the uniqueness, is the real theorem. This is still the most directly achievable of the major results because the Ludics machinery for it already exists; the work is the translation and the minimality argument.

## 3. The quantitative core: one confidence algebra

The quantitative layer needs to be rebuilt, and before anything else this thrust has to close a fork the current notes leave open — whether confidence is *semiring-valued* (the graded-Ludics picture immediately below) or *quantale-valued* (the enrichment picture after it). These are different algebraic commitments competing for the same slot, and only one should win: the semiring buys the clean Boolean/probability/tropical instances and the weighted-relational-models lineage, while the quantale buys the Lawvere enrichment and a *derived* submultiplicativity. Resolving that choice is the first task here, not a detail. The Dempster-Shafer evidence aggregation is the theoretically weakest component — DS combination is famously ill-behaved (Zadeh's counterexample, the normalization pathologies, non-associativity), and no logician would let it stand. The principled replacement is a *semiring-graded Ludics*: designs carry weights in a commutative semiring, and interaction computes a semiring-valued convergence degree. Pick the Boolean semiring and you recover classical "does it converge"; pick the probability monad or the tropical semiring and you get graded confidence, with the equity metrics falling out as semiring computations over the interaction rather than being bolted on. This rides existing work on quantitative semantics of linear logic (weighted relational models, the Laird–Manzonetto–McCusker–Pagani line) and would unify the qualitative and quantitative into one algebraic object. The deep payoff is that this collapses the distinction between *proof* and *argument* **(open conjecture — not to be assumed)**: a mathematical proof is just the Boolean-graded fragment of a defeasible deliberation, an argument that happens to converge with weight 1 against every opponent. Classical proof becomes a special case of graded collective reasoning. Collaborative mathematical proof is multi-party Ludics interaction at Boolean grade, ordinary deliberation is the same interaction at a softer grade. Treat that last claim as a slogan to be *earned*, not assumed: proof has structure — cut-elimination, a canonical normal form, decidable checkability — that ordinary deliberation provably lacks, and the Boolean-grade collapse recovers "converges or not" while potentially losing exactly the structure that makes proof *proof*. The slogan is cashed out only by the cut-elimination theorem below, not by the grading alone.

The confidence algebra is the most tractable place to get a clean, genuinely new result. The three confidence modes (min, product, Dempster-Shafer) are currently implemented side by side without a unifying theory. The natural move is to recognize that the requirements he already imposes — composition is submultiplicative (c(g∘f) ≥ c(g)•c(f)), join is a semilattice homomorphism, identity is top — are close to the axioms of a quantale: a complete lattice with an associative product that distributes over arbitrary joins, which is exactly the algebra of resources that combine multiplicatively and aggregate by supremum. The conjecture worth proving is that confidence measures should be quantale-valued, with min mode, product mode, and a DS-style structure as three instances, and — the unifying result — that an Isonomia deliberation is a category enriched over the confidence quantale **(open conjecture — not to be assumed)**, in the precise sense in which Lawvere showed metric spaces are categories enriched over [0,∞]. The hom-sets don't just carry confidence; the enrichment forces composition to respect the quantale multiplication, which is the submultiplicativity, derived rather than imposed. State the status honestly, though: this is a conjecture with a strong aesthetic, not a corollary. The enrichment only *forces* the axioms you want if composition genuinely respects the quantale product on the nose, and whether the actual `compose` does so is exactly the kind of thing that looks inevitable on the page and breaks on contact with the implementation. If it holds, that single reframing absorbs the Ambler categorical layer and the confidence layer into one structure. The hard part is Dempster-Shafer, because Dempster's combination rule misbehaves under high conflict (the Zadeh counterexample), and a research program would have to either prove the misbehavior is neutralized by the fact that conflict in argumentation is explicitly represented as attacks rather than hidden in the mass assignment, or replace the rule with a quantale-valued or imprecise-probability operation and prove its monotonicity and continuity. The proof-theoretic crown of this thrust would be a graded substructural logic — a refinement of linear logic with a confidence-graded exponential modality !_r, in the lineage of bounded linear logic and the current graded-modal-type work — that is sound and complete for the deliberation semantics, with a cut-elimination theorem. Cut-elimination would be the formal content of resolving a deliberation: normalizing the argument network is eliminating the cuts, and the normal form is the settled state. That is a major but well-posed undertaking, and it's the result that would make the system a logic and not just a data structure.

## 4. Distributed semantics: the sheaf-theoretic cohomology of disagreement

This is the program's deepest opportunity, and it braids two genuinely distinct programs that should be *sequenced*, not presented as co-equal: a conservative bicategory-coherence result that is prerequisite hygiene, and the high-ceiling sheaf-cohomology bet that depends on it. Take the hygiene first. The Plexus is a 2-category — deliberations as objects, transport functors as 1-morphisms, natural transformations left as explicit future work — and transport is restricted to one hop "by contract" because we won't fake functor composition we can't guarantee. The principled lift is a coherence theorem for the Plexus bicategory characterizing exactly when transport composes A→B→C without semantic drift; you cannot honestly talk about gluing over a site until composition of covers is coherent, which is precisely why this comes first.

With coherence in hand, the deeper and riskier direction is sheaf-theoretic: a categorical, and specifically sheaf-theoretic, semantics of distributed deliberation. The platform is primed for it — the Plexus network with "transport across deliberations" and "cross-room functors" is functoriality stated in plain English, and Ludics has known categorical models (Faggian and Hyland's work on designs as a category, the *-autonomous structure), so the objects-are-issues, morphisms-are-arguments, duality-is-the-proponent/opponent-flip picture is within reach. The move that matters is to treat the network of local deliberations as a *site* — a category whose covering families are the transport relationships — and the local argument structures as a sheaf (a stack, really, since it's a 2-category) over it, then ask the gluing question: when do locally-reached agreements glue into a global one? In general they don't — and the obstruction to gluing, the cohomology of the sheaf, is exactly the formal measure of irreducible, non-local disagreement: not "these rooms disagree" but the topological character of why the disagreement cannot be resolved locally. This is not a fantasy direction; it is the exact structure Abramsky used to render quantum contextuality as an obstruction to global sections (local consistency, global inconsistency, the obstruction living in a cohomology group), since ported to databases and distributed inference, and Isonomia's cross-room architecture is a natural home for it. A cohomological invariant that quantifies "this disagreement cannot be resolved by any redistribution of local agreements, it is a genuine global obstruction" would be the single most striking theoretical export the platform could produce, and it would matter far beyond the platform — it also vindicates the whole stance, since the point was never to force consensus but to locate disagreement precisely, and "the disagreement is a non-trivial cohomology class" is the exact formal content of "this is a real disagreement, not a misunderstanding."

## 5. Mechanization

The proof-assistant work is both the most immediately tractable and the most underrated. They've already machine-checked individual conjectures in Agda (the T002 antichain result, the C004 saturation-closure result), which means they know the discipline. The next level is a full formalization of the Ludics core — associativity of interaction, the separation theorem, internal completeness — in a dependent type theory, which essentially does not exist in the literature. That's a contribution to the proof-theory community entirely independent of the platform; people have wanted a mechanized Ludics for twenty years. Alongside it sits a type-theoretic treatment of the Walton schemes: a scheme is a typed inference rule whose critical questions are its elimination obligations, an argument instancing a scheme is a term of the corresponding dependent type, and the question the program already confronted in resolving the C006/C007/C008 trilemma — *when are two schemes identical?* — becomes a univalence-flavored question, schemes are equal exactly when they're behaviourally equivalent. The resolution “scheme identity is behaviour-extensional," is the inferentialist's version of "equivalence is equality," and making that precise in a HoTT setting would tie the argumentation layer to the foundations of mathematics in a non-gratuitous way.

## 6. The philosophy bridge

Underneath all of it sits the philosophy bridge, which is less about new theorems than about keeping the formalism honest: a soundness result relating the platform's commitment-and-entitlement tracking to Brandom's deontic scorekeeping (proving the commitment store does what inferentialist semantics says it should), and an operationalization of Negarestani's "equality of all minds" as a symmetry/anonymity axiom on the interaction, with the consequences of that axiom worked out — which is also what feeds the impossibility/realizability result anticipated in direction 1 (the characterization of which argumentation semantics are interactively realizable).

## Decisions to resolve before this becomes a plan

Three forks shape several directions at once. The first is deferred to dedicated work; the other two are settled here.

- **Semiring vs. quantale for confidence — RESOLVED (session of 2026-06-02): adopt the log-odds semiring, replace DS, park the enrichment prize.** The dedicated session worked the fork against the actual reducers in [evidential/route.ts](../app/api/deliberations/[id]/evidential/route.ts) and reached a concrete conclusion rather than a preference. The finding that decided it: the code implements *two* algebras tagged by `mode`, not one — `min` (compose `=min`, join `=max`) is a genuine distributive tropical/Gödel **quantale** but is **idempotent**, so it does *not* stack corroboration; `product` (compose `=×`, join `=` noisy-OR $1-\prod(1-x)$) *does* stack but **is not a semiring at all** — product does not distribute over noisy-OR (counterexample $h=0.5, f=0.6, g=0.7$: $h\circ(f\vee g)=0.44 \neq 0.545=(h\circ f)\vee(h\circ g)$), so it silently violates the distributivity law the architecture doc claims (§2.2, law 5) and cannot host the enrichment prize. The resolution:
  - **Adopt a log-odds / weight-of-evidence semiring.** Map confidence $c$ to $w=\log\frac{c}{1-c}\in\mathbb{R}$; corroboration is *addition* in $\mathbb{R}$ (unbounded, associative, commutative, identity $0$ = confidence $0.5$ = "no evidence"). This is the lawful realization of "corroboration stacks" — *not* the existing noisy-OR, which was the unprincipled middle that broke distributivity.
  - **Replace Dempster-Shafer outright.** Pro/con become signed evidence ($w_+ + w_-$); conflict is just addition of opposite signs. This dissolves the Zadeh high-conflict pathology, removes Dempster normalization and non-associativity, and retires the separate `NegationMap` conflict-mass band.
  - **Reclassify `min` mode** as the distributive-quantale instance and the *only* enrichment-ready branch — kept as a separate skeptical projection, not the default confidence semantics.
  - **Deprecate `product`/noisy-OR** as algebraically unsound (distributivity fails; not a semiring).
  - **Accept the cost, on the record:** the log-odds semiring is non-idempotent, so the Lawvere-enrichment prize (which needs an idempotent complete-lattice join — i.e. the quantale, i.e. `min`) is **parked, not pursued**, consistent with its "open conjecture — not to be assumed" status. Choosing stacking corroboration means declining the structure the enrichment conjecture would enrich over.
- **Conjecture, not premise — settled.** The two most beautiful claims — the Lawvere-style enrichment ("an Isonomia deliberation is a category enriched over the confidence quantale") and "classical proof is the Boolean-graded fragment of deliberation" — are to be treated as **conjectures at most, never as established facts or working premises.** They are the most likely to break: the enrichment on contact with the real `compose`, the proof-collapse on contact with the proof theory. They are the *prize*, not the *premise*. No argument, design decision, or downstream result may assume either one until the enrichment is checked against the actual implementation and the cut-elimination theorem is in hand. Until then they are clearly labelled as open conjectures wherever they appear.
- **Plexus sequencing — settled: coherence first, sheaf-cohomology after.** Bicategory coherence (the hygiene result) strictly precedes the sheaf-cohomology export. The latter is not even well-posed until composition of covers is coherent, so the two are sequential, not parallel: do coherence first, and only once it lands open the sheaf-theoretic work. Resourcing should reflect that hard dependency. *The operational reason (Session 07, 2026-06-07): holonomy around $A\to B\to C\to A$ is undefined until $A\to B\to C$ composition is coherent — the sequencing is literally "define the connection before computing its curvature." The scoping for this direction (one-hop contract reframed as a provenance guardrail not a categorical wall; first cohomology target = $\mathbb{R}$-valued discrete holonomy on the Plexus graph; empirical topology study to check the live graph isn't a forest) lives in [`10_IDEATION_SESSIONS/07-distributed-semantics-sheaf-cohomology-2026-06-07.md`](10_IDEATION_SESSIONS/07-distributed-semantics-sheaf-cohomology-2026-06-07.md).*

The through-line is worth restating because it is the program's best feature: every direction takes something the platform currently *asserts* and asks for the theorem that would make it a *consequence*. The agenda is strong precisely where it names its own obstructions — preferred-semantics maximality, Dempster-Shafer under high conflict, functor composition we won't fake — rather than papering over them. Keep that discipline and the rest follows.
