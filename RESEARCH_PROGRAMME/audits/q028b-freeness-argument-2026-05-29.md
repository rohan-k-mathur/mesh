## 0. Headline

The freeness/adjointness argument lands as a reduction:

> **Reduction theorem.** Fix $(\Gamma, A\text{-pres}, \text{pol-align}, \delta)$. For every Ludics behaviour $B$ representable under $(\Gamma, \delta)$ as an Ambler formula $B^\sharp$, the map
> $$\phi : \mathsf{Inc}(B) \to \mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp), \qquad D \mapsto \delta^{-1}(\mathrm{CH}(\mathrm{DP}(D)))$$
> is a canonical bijection. Its free-JSL extension $\varepsilon_{A,B}$ is therefore a canonical JSL iso, closing C001b′ b₁′ ∧ b₂′ *modulo the four side-data items*.

DP = design-as-proof (Fouqueré–Quatrini 2013); CH = Curry–Howard; $\delta^{-1}$ = inverse of the defeat-encoding on defeat actions.

The four side-data items, in order of triviality:

1. **$\Gamma$** — defeasible rule-base. Required because Ludics $B$ does not carry one. Conventional; should be a parameter of any responsible bridge statement.
2. **$A$-presentation** — typing-context presentation. Quotient-trivial under $\alpha$-equivalence + exchange.
3. **polarity-alignment** — Ludics focusing $\leftrightarrow$ Ambler evaluation strategy. Fixed once globally by Ambler 1996 §2 + Girard *Locus Solum*; mutually compatible.
4. **$\delta$ — defeat-encoding** — the **substantive** choice. At minimum two non-equivalent options (defeat-as-negation vs defeat-as-coroutine); substrate has not committed.

## 1. Setup

The bridge target (from Q-027 audit §7, ratified by C001b′):

$$\varepsilon_{A,B} : \mathcal{P}_\mathrm{fin}(\mathsf{Inc}(B)) \to \mathcal{C}_\mathrm{semi}(A, B^\sharp).$$

For Ambler's worked example $\mathcal{S}/\Gamma$, the right-hand side is $\mathcal{P}_\mathrm{fin}(\mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp))$ — finite sets of base $\lambda$-terms under union.

Both sides are free JSLs (with $\bot$) on canonical generating sets:

- LHS generated by $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$, canonical by T002 + Fouqueré–Quatrini 2013 incarnation uniqueness.
- RHS generated by $\mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp)$, canonical by Ambler's definition.

**Categorical fact.** Maps between free JSLs are determined by their restriction to generators (Mac Lane *CWM* IV.1). Therefore $\varepsilon$ is iso iff the generator-restriction $\phi$ is bijection. So:

$$\text{C001b}'\;\text{b}_1' \wedge \text{b}_2' \iff \phi : \mathsf{Inc}(B) \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp).$$

Q-028b asks whether $\phi$ is forced by the adjunction $F \dashv U$ restricted to generators, or merely existential.

## 2. Step 1 — Is $A_\Gamma$ canonical given $B$?

The critical intermediate question named in Q-028b's `next-action`.

Read literally: does $B$ alone pick out a unique Ambler argument-type $B^\sharp$?

**No, and the failure is informative.** $B$ is bi-orthogonally closed on a Ludics base; it does not carry $\Gamma$. The bridge is unavoidably $(\Gamma, B) \mapsto B^\sharp_\Gamma$. This is not a defect — it is the bridge revealing what data it actually requires. C001b′ should be stated parametrically in $\Gamma$.

Inside a fixed $\Gamma$, $B^\sharp$ is determined by the propositional content of $B$ up to logical equivalence in $\Gamma$'s logical fragment. That's "canonical up to canonical iso" — the standard tolerance of any categorical statement.

→ **Side-data item 1: $\Gamma$.**

## 3. Step 2 — Same question for $A$

$\mathcal{S}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp)$ is parameterised by two objects; $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$ knows about $B$ only.

Resolution: the Ludics-side relevant set is not $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$ in isolation but $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$ *relative to a presentation of $A$ as a typing context*. For propositional $A$, the presentation is canonical (declare a fresh variable $x : A$). For higher types, the presentation involves $\alpha$-renaming and ordering choices that are quotient-trivial under MALL exchange + $\alpha$-equivalence.

→ **Side-data item 2: $A$-presentation.** Conventional, quotient-trivial.

## 4. Step 3 — Polarity / focusing alignment

Ludics has strict polarity (positive/negative loci, focusing) from *Locus Solum*. Ambler's $\lambda$-calculus has implicit polarity from its evaluation strategy. For the bijection to respect evaluation order, the two must agree.

Both Ambler 1996 §2 and Girard 2001 fix mutually compatible conventions (intuitionistic-in-linear style), but the compatibility is a fact one invokes, not a definition. Once fixed globally, it does not need to be re-chosen per behaviour.

→ **Side-data item 3: polarity-alignment.** Fixed once; not re-chosen per $(A, B)$.

## 5. Step 4 — Defeasibility encoding (the substantive choice)

Ambler's $\lambda$-calculus is **not** linear. It admits structural rules (weakening, contraction) and includes a *defeat operator* (Ambler 1996 §3). Girard's design-as-proof correspondence is established for linear logic (Fouqueré–Quatrini 2013, MALL fragment). To use DP as a bridge ingredient, the defeasible/intuitionistic fragment must be encoded linearly.

- **Structural part:** the standard Girard 1987 translation $A \to B := \;!A \multimap B$ is conventional and well-defined.
- **Defeat operator:** at least two reasonable encodings exist:
  1. **Defeat-as-negation.** An Ambler defeat $\mathrm{defeat}(\pi)$ becomes a negative design (Daimon ramification with explicit attack ramification) — the defeated argument becomes a "blocked" chronicle on the negative side. Matches Phase 2e's Cross-Cone Incompatibility interpretation.
  2. **Defeat-as-coroutine.** An Ambler defeat becomes a positive locus marked for cross-cone interaction — the defeated argument is a parallel "challenger" computation. Matches a CSP-style operational reading.

These are **not equivalent** and produce different bijections on $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$ for any $B$ that involves defeat (i.e. any non-trivial defeasible behaviour). The substrate has not committed to either.

→ **Side-data item 4: $\delta$, the defeat-encoding.** **The first genuinely non-trivial choice.**

## 6. Step 5 — The reduction theorem (proof sketch)

**Proposition (Q-028b reduction).** Fix $(\Gamma, A\text{-pres}, \text{pol-align}, \delta)$. Let $B$ be a Ludics behaviour representable under $(\Gamma, \delta)$ as an Ambler formula $B^\sharp$. The map

$$\phi : \mathsf{Inc}(B) \to \mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp), \qquad D \mapsto \delta^{-1}(\mathrm{CH}(\mathrm{DP}(D)))$$

is a canonical bijection.

### 6.1 Injection

Take $D \neq D' \in \mathsf{Inc}(B)$. They differ in at least one action (chronicle position).

1. **DP step.** By Fouqueré–Quatrini 2013 §4.2, the incarnation $|B|$ is *exactly* the set of cut-free MALL proofs of (the linear shadow of) $B$, quotiented by permutation of independent rules. Different equivalence classes ↔ different incarnations. So $\mathrm{DP}(D) \neq \mathrm{DP}(D')$ in the cut-free-proof quotient.
2. **CH step.** Distinct cut-free MALL proofs (up to permutation) decode to distinct $\beta$-normal linear $\lambda$-terms (up to $\alpha$). Girard–Lafont–Taylor *Proofs and Types* ch. 8 (the CH iso for MALL/MELL is faithful on the cut-free / $\beta$-normal fragments).
3. **$\delta^{-1}$ step.** Injective by construction (side-data item 4 commits to an injection on defeat actions).

Composite $\phi$ is injective.

### 6.2 Surjection

Take $t \in \mathcal{C}/\Gamma(A, B^\sharp)$ — an $\alpha\beta\eta$-class of Ambler $\lambda$-terms typeable as $A \to B^\sharp$ in $\Gamma$.

1. **$\delta$ step.** Translate $t$ via $\delta$ to a linear-logic $\lambda$-term $\delta(t)$ in the $!$-translation of $\Gamma$.
2. **CH step.** By completeness of Curry–Howard, $\delta(t)$ corresponds to a unique (up to permutation) cut-free MALL proof of the linear shadow of $A \vdash B^\sharp$.
3. **DP step.** By FQ 2013 §4.2 (the other direction), this proof corresponds to a design in $|B|$ — i.e. an element of $\mathsf{Inc}(B)$.

$\phi$ is surjective.

### 6.3 Canonicality

Each step in the chain is canonical *given the side data*:

- DP: unique up to permutation of independent rules (FQ 2013 §4.2 establishes that the equivalence class is precisely the incarnation).
- CH: unique modulo $\beta\eta$ (Girard–Lafont–Taylor *Proofs and Types* ch. 8).
- $\delta^{-1}$: unique by side-data item 4.

The non-uniqueness lives entirely in the choice of side data; the bijection $\phi$ is forced once side data is fixed. ∎

## 7. Verdict and implications

**Q-028b: qualified positive.** The bijection is forced given the four side-data items.

## 9. Open obstructions / things worth stress-testing before promoting

1. **The DP step's MALL restriction.** FQ 2013 §4.2 is for MALL. Ambler's $\lambda$-calculus uses MELL ($!$-translated intuitionistic). The extension of DP from MALL to MELL is folklore but I have not verified it from a primary source in this session. If the MELL extension fails or requires non-trivial conditions, the reduction needs tightening. Recommend checking FQ 2013 §6 (extensions) or successor papers.
2. **The $\delta^{-1}$ "injective by construction" clause.** Both candidate $\delta$'s are injective for trivially-defeasible $B$ (no defeat). Whether they remain injective once defeat chains are allowed (Ambler's `defeat(defeat(π))`) is a separate small check. Recommend verifying on the aspirin Q3 case in the Q-027 audit, which involves one defeat layer (`¬aspirin` from `gu`).
3. **Polarity-alignment vacuity.** The claim "Ambler 1996 + Girard 2001 are mutually compatible" was asserted from memory of the substrate doc, not verified against either primary source in this session. If they are not mutually compatible, side-data item 3 escalates to substantive.
4. **The cardinality-2 ambiguity carried over from Q-027.** Q3 aspirin's 2-element bijection is consistent with both candidate $\delta$'s producing the same $\phi$ (any function between 2-element sets works). The reduction's *force* needs verification on a case where $|\mathsf{Inc}(B)| \geq 3$ — this is exactly what Q-028a stratum 1 will provide as cross-check.

These are stress tests, not blockers. Recommend filing them as `next-action` substeps on Q-028b's revised entry rather than as new questions.
