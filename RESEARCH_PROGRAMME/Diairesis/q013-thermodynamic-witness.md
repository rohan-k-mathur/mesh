# Q-013 §3 — The Thermodynamic-Arrow Witness (corroboration grade)

*The first witness of the §3 hunt: not a tertium-candidate but external corroboration of
Horn B. Does physics, working independently, find the arrow* generated *— or* posited /
presupposed*? Run against Roberts,* Reversing the Arrow of Time *(2022), chs. 5–7, plus the
Callender SEP entry. Conservative use throughout: the insufficiency/independence core only,
not Roberts's distinctive positive thesis (recorded as a research seam in §5).*

> **Verdict.** **Corroborates Horn B, decisively, and sharpens it.** Physics did not generate
> the arrow from non-arrow materials in any of the candidate sites. The macroscopic/thermodynamic
> arrow is *not lawlike directedness* — Roberts argues classical thermodynamics is temporally
> symmetric in all but a trivial way (Ch. 6), and the statistical-mechanical arrow rests on the
> Past Hypothesis, a posited low-entropy boundary condition (Ch. 5; Callender's "bald posit").
> The one genuinely lawlike micro-asymmetry — electroweak T-violation (Ch. 7) — is a *localized
> Standard-Model feature*, categorically distinct from and insufficient for the thermodynamic
> arrow. Neither site shows the arrow being *generated*. The two physics-irreducibility stories
> (posited boundary condition; primitive lawlike asymmetry) disagree on the *mode* of
> irreducibility but agree it is **not generated** — which is exactly what the dilemma predicts.

---

## 1. What the witness is for

This is corroboration, not a tertium-hunt. The dilemma (§2 of the approach) predicts: any
account of self-differentiation either can't generate the arrow (Horn A, structural/static) or
presupposes it (Horn B, temporal/processual). If that is a deep fact and not an artifact of the
(co)algebra paradigm, then a field that has studied the arrow directly for a century — the
physics of the arrow of time — should independently have reached "posited or presupposed, not
generated." It has. The value here is that this verdict was reached with entirely different
motivations and tools, so convergence is evidence the dilemma is not parochial.

## 2. The premise physics supplies for free: the dynamics are time-symmetric

The reversibility (Loschmidt) result is the physics statement of "the operation is arrow-neutral"
(Check A's finding, now in mechanics): every known classical motion has a time-reversed motion
that is equally allowed, so a time-symmetric microdynamics that runs low→high entropy has a
counterpart running anti-thermodynamically. The fundamental dynamics carry no arrow. Any arrow
must therefore come from *elsewhere* — boundary conditions or a lawlike non-invariance. This is
precisely the fork Horn A vs Horn B formalizes, reached independently.

## 3. Leg one — the thermodynamic/macroscopic arrow is not *generated* (Ch. 5–6)

Two layers, both corroborating Horn B / boundary-condition:

- **Statistical-mechanical arrow → a posited boundary condition (Ch. 5).** Boltzmann's counting
  argument is time-symmetric: it gives equally good reason to expect higher entropy to the future
  *and* to the past. To get a one-way arrow one restricts to a low-entropy past — the Past
  Hypothesis — which is a *posit*, not a derivation. Callender's SEP entry states the consensus in
  the literature's own voice: absent new physics or a satisfactory third way, one is left with a
  bald posit of special initial conditions, and temporally asymmetric boundary conditions are
  needed to solve the problem. Even Earman, the harshest critic of the Past Hypothesis, grants the
  boundary-condition point while attacking the rest — which makes the corroboration *stronger*,
  not weaker: the contested part is the posit's status, not the fact that an asymmetric posit is
  required. This is "imported, not generated" in physics.
- **Classical thermodynamics carries no arrow at all (Ch. 6).** Roberts sharpens the claim past
  "imported": classical thermodynamics, the paradigm time-asymmetric theory, is on his analysis
  temporally symmetric in all but a trivial way. If right, the macroscopic arrow is not merely a
  boundary-condition import on top of a lawlike directedness — there is *no lawlike thermodynamic
  directedness to begin with*. This is the strongest possible corroboration of "the arrow is not
  in the structure": the structure here is the thermodynamic theory itself, and it has no arrow.

Net: at the macroscopic level the arrow is either a posited boundary condition (Ch. 5) or absent
from the theory entirely (Ch. 6). Nowhere is it *generated* from non-arrow materials.

## 4. Leg two — CP/T-violation is real, but localized and insufficient (Ch. 7)

The pressure point: physics has *one* built-in microscopic time-asymmetry, and the witness must
show it neither is the thermodynamic arrow nor was itself generated.

- **It is real.** Cronin–Fitch (1964): the long-lived neutral kaon decays to two pions, violating
  CP; via the CPT theorem, CP-violation implies T-violation. Later confirmed directly (CPLEAR,
  B-mesons, lepton sector). So there is a genuine lawlike micro non-invariance under time reversal.
- **It is localized and small.** It lives in the electroweak (weak) sector, as a feature of
  specific decay processes — a *Standard-Model parameter* (the CKM/CP-violating phase), not a
  pervasive directedness.
- **It is categorically distinct from, and insufficient for, the thermodynamic arrow.** This is
  the decisive leg. The thermodynamic arrow (Ch. 5–6) and the electroweak T-violation (Ch. 7) are
  *different things*: the former is a boundary-condition / macroscopic matter, the latter a lawlike
  micro non-invariance in a specific interaction. The T-violation does not yield entropy increase;
  the Past Hypothesis is still required regardless of it. So the one micro-asymmetry physics has
  does **not** generate the macroscopic arrow — exactly the insufficiency the witness needed.

**Honesty flag (load-bearing, do not bury).** The word "posited" applies cleanly to the
*thermodynamic* arrow (the Past Hypothesis) but **Roberts would not apply it to the electroweak
asymmetry** — for him the T-violation is *lawlike and primitive*, the one genuine arrow, not a
posit. The conservative witness therefore rests leg two on **localization + insufficiency** (which
Roberts fully supports), *not* on calling the electroweak asymmetry "posited" (which he denies).
The leg stands on what the text supports: T-violation is a distinct, localized, lawlike electroweak
phenomenon that does not deliver the thermodynamic arrow.

## 5. The refinement, and the research seam (b)

Reading Roberts straight changes one thing in the test shape, recorded here rather than buried:
**Roberts is himself a Horn-A–flavoured challenger.** His positive thesis (Ch. 7, via the
Representation View of chs. 2–4) is that electroweak T-violation is genuine, *lawlike*,
non-posited evidence for an arrow of *time itself*. In our terms this is the claim that
fundamental physics contains a directedness that is **neither imported (boundary condition) nor
presupposed (temporal process) but simply primitive and lawlike**.

This does *not* break the dilemma. It is not a tertium: it does not *generate* the arrow from
non-arrow materials — it *finds* the arrow already lawlike in the electroweak sector. So it
corroborates the predicted FAIL (the arrow is irreducible) while relocating *where* the
irreducibility sits. The upshot is that physics offers **two** irreducibility stories, and
neither is generation:

| | mode of irreducibility | generated from non-arrow materials? |
|---|---|---|
| Thermodynamic arrow (Ch. 5–6) | posited boundary condition (Past Hypothesis) / or no arrow at all | No |
| Electroweak arrow (Ch. 7, Roberts) | primitive *lawlike* asymmetry of the weak interaction | No |

Both land on "not generated." They disagree only on the *mode* — posited vs primitive-lawlike —
which is a refinement *within* Horn-B-style irreducibility, not an escape from it.

**Research seam (b), recorded for later, not on the load-bearing path.** Engage Roberts's positive
thesis head-on: is "primitive lawlike asymmetry" a genuinely distinct *third* mode of the arrow's
irreducibility — neither posited-boundary-condition nor presupposed-process — and if so, does it
bear on Q-016 (would a *lawlike* arrow change the distinction/arrow priority order)? This commits
to taking a position on the Representation View (time reversal = reversal of time translations;
the antiunitary `T`; the symmetry-existence criterion), which is more contested and which the
conservative witness deliberately keeps out of the load-bearing path. Flagged as the most
promising single extension of the physics leg.

## 6. What the witness establishes, and its bound

- **Establishes:** physics, independently, finds the arrow *posited or primitive-lawlike, never
  generated* — corroborating Horn B and the predicted FAIL (arrow irreducible). The thermodynamic
  arrow is imported or absent (Ch. 5–6); the one lawlike micro-asymmetry is localized and
  insufficient for it (Ch. 7). Convergence of an independent century-long literature on "not
  generated" is strong evidence the dilemma is not an artifact of the (co)algebra paradigm.
- **Bound:** this is corroboration, not proof. It cannot close Q-013 by itself — it shows physics
  *found no generation* of the arrow, not that generation is impossible. The closing argument
  remains the dilemma's exhaustiveness (§2 of the approach), to which this is external support.
- **Does not touch:** the remaining live risk in §3 is **symmetry breaking** — the candidate that
  might generate orientation *structurally* (in a ground-state manifold) rather than by a temporal
  process. The thermodynamic witness does not address it; spontaneous symmetry breaking appears in
  Roberts only as a representation-failure phenomenon (Ch. 4), not as a generator of the arrow. The
  symmetry-breaking pre-study stands as the next and last real test of the dilemma.
