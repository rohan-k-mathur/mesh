## §0 — The distinctive difficulty

Every prior test had a *concrete operation* to run (re-entry, the stream functor). Q-013 has a
*universal quantifier* over an open-ended space: "any account of self-differentiation." You
cannot settle it by exhibiting one operation — you need either (a) one non-fixed-point witness
that generates the arrow (flips it to "reducible"), or (b) an argument that *every* account
must fail to generate it. Enumeration cannot deliver (b). So the approach must convert the
universal into a *dilemma argument* (§2) plus a *witness-hunt* (§3); the strength of any
"irreducible tout court" verdict will ride on the dilemma, not on the survey.

---

## §1 — Well-formedness precondition: a paradigm-neutral genus of self-differentiation

Before asking whether non-fixed-point self-differentiation generates the arrow, we need a
notion of "self-differentiation" that is not already fixed-point-shaped — otherwise the
question is rigged or empty.

- **Task.** State the *genus* of which `X = F(X)` self-reference is one *species*. Working
  proposal: **self-differentiation = any structure in which the differentiating and the
  differentiated coincide** — where what does the distinguishing and what gets distinguished are
  the same. Fixed-point self-reference is the species where "coincide" = "is a fixed point of."
  Other species would realize "coincide" differently (a process that is its own product; a
  negation that negates itself; a field that differentiates itself).
- **The precondition is itself a result.** If no paradigm-neutral genus can be stated — if
  "self-differentiation in general" is not a determinate notion — then Q-013 is **aporetic**:
  the arrow's irreducibility-tout-court is undecidable because its domain ("all accounts") is
  ill-formed. *Establishing or failing to establish the genus is the first deliverable, and
  either outcome is informative.* (This is the I6 guard applied to Q-013 itself.)

## §2 — The reduction: Q-013 turns on one fork (the dilemma of generation)

The spine of the approach. "To *generate* the arrow" can mean only one of two things, and
each horn has already been shown (or is structurally bound) to fail:

- **Horn A — atemporal / structural generation** (a definition, a construction, a proof, a
  supervenience): this *is* the fixed-point / static paradigm. Checks B and C established it
  **imports** the arrow (ω-index, completeness, the NNO axiom, the `μ/ν` selection) rather than
  generating it. A static structure has no arrow to give; it can only have one posited on it.
- **Horn B — temporal / processual generation** (a process that brings the arrow about by
  unfolding): any such process is itself *directed* — it has a before and an after — so it
  **presupposes** the arrow it is meant to generate. You cannot run a generating-process without
  the directedness that is the arrow.

If these two horns are **exhaustive**, the arrow is irreducible tout court: no account generates
directionality from non-directional materials, because structural generation can't and processual
generation already assumes it. The transcendental form: *the arrow is a condition of any
generation whatever, so it cannot be the output of one* — exactly the shape of the descent's L−1
move ("you can't deny distinction without enacting it"), now: "you can't generate the arrow
without a process that uses it."

**So Q-013 reduces to a single question:**

> **Is there a third sense of "generate" — neither atemporal-structural nor
> temporal-processual — under which the arrow could be produced from non-arrow materials?**

That is the whole test. Everything else is the hunt for such a tertium.

**Robustness check on the dichotomy** (candidate tertia, pre-screened): *emergence* collapses
into A (synchronic/supervenience) or B (diachronic/process); *stochastic* generation is B and
yields no direction (randomness is symmetric); *modal/necessary* generation is A; *self-bootstrapping*
is A (fixed point) or B (temporal) — the Tathandlung is circular, not a third way. None has so
far survived as a genuine tertium. The witness-hunt (§3) is where a real one would have to appear.

Each candidate is a place a tertium might hide. Pre-classification by horn, with the open edge
flagged. Sources marked ✓ read / ◦ queued.

## §4 — Decision procedure and pass/fail

For each candidate: (i) is it genuinely non-fixed-point, or does it reduce to `X = F(X)`? (ii)
which horn — does it *import* the arrow (A) or *presuppose* it (B)? (iii) if it claims to escape
both, exhibit the tertium sense of "generate" precisely.

- **PASS (arrow reducible tout court):** one non-fixed-point account *generates* the arrow from
  non-arrow materials — a tertium sense of generation, surviving the smuggle-criterion (output >
  input, no imported directedness, no presupposed process). One witness suffices.
- **FAIL (arrow irreducible tout court):** the dilemma (§2) is exhaustive — every account falls
  into Horn A (imports) or Horn B (presupposes) — corroborated by the survey finding no tertium.
  The two-primitive base becomes paradigm-*independent*.
- **APORETIC:** the genus (§1) cannot be stated; "all accounts of self-differentiation" is not a
  determinate domain; the irreducibility-tout-court is undecidable. (Then the honest terminus is
  the *paradigm-relative* result plus a defended account of why the universal form is ill-posed.)

**Honest limit.** The universal claim cannot be closed by enumeration; a FAIL verdict rests on
the dilemma argument's exhaustiveness, which the survey can corroborate but not prove. So the
realistic strongest outcome is: *FAIL, conditional on the dilemma being exhaustive, with no
tertium found among the serious candidates* — a well-supported but in-principle-defeasible
universal, defeasible exactly by a future tertium.

## §5 — Predicted verdict (flagged as prediction)

**FAIL — the arrow is irreducible tout court — via the dilemma, with the physics of the arrow of
time as decisive corroboration.** Prediction, because §3 is not yet run and the Hegelian
determinate-negation claim deserves a real hearing before the dichotomy is declared exhaustive.
The transcendental spine (the arrow is the condition of any generation, hence not its output) is
robust to the "which account" question because it concerns the *form of generation*, not any
particular operation; its one vulnerability is the same as L−1's — it shows the arrow is a
condition of *generation as we can mean it*, leaving a sliver for a tertium sense no one has yet
exhibited. The witness-hunt is precisely the search of that sliver.
