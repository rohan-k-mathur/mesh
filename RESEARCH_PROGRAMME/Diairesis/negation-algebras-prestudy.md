# Pre-study — The Three Negation-Algebras

*Bounded formalization, ahead of running Q-013's §3. One question: can determinate negation
be given a non-collapsing (`¬¬A ≠ A`) formalization whose non-collapse is **not** underwritten
by a pre-given order? If yes, the dilemma's Horn A is breached and §2 needs a third horn; if
no, the strongest philosophical tertium-candidate falls into Horn A and the test shape is
confirmed for formalizable accounts. Three outcomes anticipated: (1) every non-collapsing
negation imports an order → shape stands; (2) an order-free surplus exists → §2 rewrites; (3)
the recurring I6 individuation residue → route to §1's aporetic floor.*

> **Ceiling, stated up front.** Formalizing determinate negation as an *operator on
> propositions* is itself an interpretive bet a Hegelian may refuse. So this pre-study can
> establish at most: *no operator-formalization yields order-free directional surplus.* A
> refusal-to-formalize is not a Hegelian victory; it routes to the §1 genus question (the
> non-formalizable kernel), which is exactly the future master question.

---

## 1. Involutive negation (Boolean / De Morgan / and, notably, LP and R)

`¬¬A = A` by definition. **Collapses.** This is the algebra of *abstract* negation — Hegel's
"bare" negation — and it is exactly re-entry's oscillation in negation form (`¬` flips, `¬¬`
returns; the involution is reversible). No non-collapse, hence **no surplus, no directionality.**
A De Morgan involution is order-*reversing* (antitone) on its lattice, so an order may be present,
but the negation does not use it to climb — it reverses and returns. **Baseline:** the
reversible/symmetric case the whole investigation keeps meeting. No arrow.

## 2. Intuitionistic negation (Heyting algebra / closure operator)

`¬A = A → ⊥`. Here `¬¬A ≠ A` in general (the failure of double-negation elimination). So we have
**genuine non-collapse** — the first real candidate for surplus. Its exact shape:

`¬¬` is a **closure operator (nucleus)**: monotone, **inflationary** (`A ≤ ¬¬A`), **idempotent**
(`¬¬¬¬A = ¬¬A`). The "surplus" of `¬¬A` over `A` is precisely the inflationarity `A ≤ ¬¬A` — a
**climb upward in the pre-given Heyting-algebra order `≤`.**

Does the non-collapse ride on a pre-given order? **Yes, unambiguously.** A closure operator is
*defined* as a monotone-inflationary-idempotent map **on a poset**; "`¬¬A` is above `A`" has no
content without the lattice order, which is presupposed. The surplus is a position in an order
that was already there. **Horn A — order imported.**

And worse for the tertium hope: idempotence means the climb **terminates at one step** — `¬¬A` is
a *fixed point* of `¬¬`, and iterating does nothing. So intuitionistic negation delivers a
*bounded, one-step climb to a fixed point* — the static/fixed-point paradigm again, and not even
arrow-as-progression (no non-terminating accumulation). **Doubly Horn A.**

## 3. Paraconsistent negation (the most Hegel-like: dialetheic LP, and da Costa's C-systems)

The contradiction-tolerating algebras, the natural home for "contradiction drives the dialectic."
The negation splits, and *neither branch* yields directional surplus:

- **Dialetheic (Priest's LP; relevant R):** the negation is **De Morgan involutive** — `¬¬A = A`.
  **Collapses** (back to §1). The dialetheia `A ∧ ¬A` is a **static** standing state — a fixed
  point where both poles hold at once — **not a movement through** contradiction. This is the
  decisive point about formalizing Hegel as dialetheism: it delivers *stable* contradiction, not
  *progressive* sublation. The forward movement is simply **absent** from the negation. (The
  recognized gap between Priest's static true-contradictions and Hegel's dynamic ones.)
- **Da Costa C-systems:** negation is **weakened, non-involutive** — `¬¬A → A` fails. So there is
  non-collapse, but it is **deflationary** (the negation is too weak to recover `A`), a *gap*, not
  a *climb* to something richer. **No surplus, no direction.**

So in no paraconsistent system does the negation **generate** a directional climb. To get
Hegelian forward movement one must **add an order** along which the contradiction resolves
"upward" — and that added order is the arrow, **imported.**

## 4. Result

| Algebra | `¬¬` | Non-collapse? | Source of any surplus | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Involutive (Boolean/De Morgan/**LP**/R) | `¬¬A = A` | No — collapses | — (oscillation) | baseline; no arrow |
| Intuitionistic (Heyting/closure) | `A ≤ ¬¬A`, idempotent | Yes — inflationary | **pre-given lattice order**; terminates at a fixed point | **Horn A (doubly)** |
| Paraconsistent — dialetheic (LP) | involutive | No — collapses | static contradiction (fixed point) | the *movement* is absent |
| Paraconsistent — da Costa (Cₙ) | weak, non-involutive | Yes — deflationary | none (a gap, no climb) | no surplus → no arrow |

**Outcome 1 obtains.** Every non-collapsing negation either imports an order (intuitionistic
closure: the surplus is a climb in a pre-given lattice) or has no directional surplus at all
(involutive collapse; dialetheic static contradiction; da Costa deflation). **No negation-algebra
generates a directional, non-terminating, accumulating surplus from negation alone.** The
Hegelian "richer, forward" of determinate negation, operator-formalized, decomposes as: negation
(the operation, reversible) **+** an imported order to climb (the arrow). Horn A is confirmed for
the strongest philosophical tertium-candidate.

## 5. The bonus finding — the non-formalizable kernel *is* the arrow

The three algebras between them deliver exactly three things, and **none** is the dialectic's
defining feature:

- involutive → **oscillation** (reversible, no progress);
- intuitionistic closure → a **terminating** one-step climb (lands at a fixed point);
- dialetheic → **static** contradiction (both poles, no movement).

What every operator-formalization **drops** is the dialectic's *non-terminating progressive
accumulation* — Being → Nothing → Becoming → Determinate Being → … climbing without returning and
without halting, each stage retaining its predecessors. But *non-terminating progressive
accumulation* is, word for word, our definition of **the arrow** (irreversible accumulation, the
`μ`-fold direction of Q-015, the never-returning progression of the coalgebra test).

So the part of the dialectic that resists operator-formalization **is the arrow.** Hegel's
non-formalizable kernel and the program's irreducible second primitive are *the same thing*. This
is why the formalization both confirms Horn A *and* cannot capture the dialectic: the directional
accumulation is exactly what no operator supplies and exactly what the whole investigation has
found is not generated but presupposed.
