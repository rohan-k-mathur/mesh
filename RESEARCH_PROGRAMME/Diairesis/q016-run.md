# Q-016 Run — Is distinction weakly prior to the arrow, or truly co-primitive?

*The highest-stakes question in the program. The first run killed priority-of-distinction
as an* ontological *claim (bead/knot difference sub-structural → I6). Q-016 asks whether a
priority survives at the level of the* primitives: *does the operation furnish the space the
arrow merely orients (distinction weakly prior), or is the space already arrowed (co-primitive)?*

> **Verdict, first.** **PASS — distinction is weakly prior to the arrow**, and the dependence
> is *supra-structural*, witnessed concretely. In an arrow-free base (`FinSet`, the topos with
> no natural-numbers object) the operation's space of forms exists — full of cyclic,
> un-oriented forms — while **neither pole of the arrow exists** (`ℕ` and `ℕ̄` are infinite,
> absent). Conversely there is **no** setting with the arrow but not the space, because the
> arrow *is* a selection within the space (`ℕ = μ(1+X)`). The dependence runs arrow → operation,
> not operation → arrow. Crucially this **escapes I6**: unlike the bead/knot question (isomorphic
> rivals, undecidable), the two settings here are *not* isomorphic (`FinSet ≇ Set`), so the
> priority is a real structural fact, not a bookkeeping choice. **The program's load-bearing
> claim is rescued — relocated from "things are residues of relations" (dead at I6) to
> "distinction is prior to the arrow" (decided, here).**

---

## 0. The fork and the criterion

- **Positive (weakly prior):** the arrow (`μ/ν` selection) operates *on* the space the
  operation opens; the space exists without the arrow; the arrow does not exist without the
  space. Asymmetric dependence → distinction weakly prior.
- **Negative (co-primitive):** the space is *already* arrowed — its order/directedness is the
  arrow — so the two are entangled, neither prior.

**Criterion.** Exhibit (a) a setting with the operation's space but no arrow, and (b) check
whether any setting has the arrow but no space. Asymmetry decides it. And — the I6 guard — check
whether the two descriptions are inter-translatable (which would deflate the priority to
convention) or genuinely asymmetric.

---

## 1. The decisive distinction the negative horn trades on

The co-primitive horn says "the space is a *category*, categories are made of *directed
morphisms*, so the arrow is already there." This conflates two directednesses:

- **Morphism-directedness** — a map `f : X → Y` has a source and a target; a local asymmetry.
  *Every* category has this, and it is **symmetric-capable**: isomorphisms, cycles of morphisms,
  and reversible dynamics are all legal. This is the **relation-level** — furnished by the
  operation (`F(·)`, the re-entry test's relation pole).
- **The arrow proper** — well-foundedness / a global first / the `μ`/`ν` (least/greatest)
  selection / irreversibility. This is what generates the numerals, orients the asymmetry, picks
  `ℕ` from the cyclic algebras. It **rules out** cycles in its dimension.

These are not the same. The category of `1+X`-algebras *has* morphism-directedness yet is *not*
well-founded: it contains the cyclic `(ℤ/n, 0, +1)` and isomorphisms. So the space carries the
relation (morphism-directedness, from the operation) but **not** the arrow (well-foundedness),
which is the extra least/greatest selection. The negative horn's "the space is already arrowed"
is true only of morphism-directedness, which is the *relation*, not the arrow.

## 2. The witness: distinction without the arrow (`FinSet`)

Make it concrete in an **arrow-free base**. `FinSet` (finite sets) is a topos with **no
natural-numbers object** — no `ℕ`, hence no arrow.

- **The operation's space exists in `FinSet`.** `F(X) = 1 + X` is a perfectly good functor on
  `FinSet`; its category of algebras is rich — `(ℤ/n, 0, +1)`, `(1, ∗, id)`, every finite set
  with a point and an endomap. The space of forms is present, **populated entirely by cyclic /
  un-oriented forms**.
- **Neither pole of the arrow exists in `FinSet`.** The initial algebra `μ(1+X) = ℕ` and the
  final coalgebra `ν(1+X) = ℕ̄` are both *infinite* — absent from `FinSet`. The operation has no
  initial algebra and no final coalgebra here.

So `FinSet` is a real model in which **distinction's space is present and the arrow is absent**.
The space does not need the arrow.

- **No dual witness exists.** Is there a setting with the arrow but not the space? No — by
  construction. The arrow *is* `μ`/`ν`, a *selection within* the space: `ℕ` just **is** the
  initial algebra of `1+X`; characterized as an NNO, it is fixed by the recursion universal
  property, which is the initial-algebra (distinction) structure. You cannot have the
  least/greatest of a space you do not have. The arrow presupposes the space.

**Asymmetric dependence, witnessed:** `arrow → operation` (the arrow needs the space), not
`operation → arrow` (the space exists, in `FinSet`, without the arrow). **Distinction weakly
prior.**

## 3. Why this escapes I6 (the result that makes it a rescue, not another bedrock)

Every priority claim so far bottomed out at I6 because the rivals were *inter-translatable* /
isomorphic — the difference was sub-structural, a bookkeeping choice. Q-016 does not, on two
counts:

- **The settings are not isomorphic.** `FinSet ≇ Set`: one has `ℕ`, the other does not. The
  presence/absence of the arrow is a genuine *structural* difference between two settings, not a
  difference invisible to every criterion. The priority is **supra-structural** — exactly the
  region the outward-thread fitness function was hunting.
- **The two descriptions are not inter-translatable** (defeating the quantifier-variance
  deflation). "Operation-first" and "arrow-first" are **not** symmetric: from the operation
  (distinction) you generate the *whole space* — all the algebras, cyclic ones included — *and*
  the arrow as one selection (`ℕ = μ`). From the arrow (`ℕ`) alone you generate **only `ℕ`** —
  one point of the space, not the space (the cyclic `ℤ/n` are not recoverable from `ℕ`).
  Operation-first **strictly dominates** in generative power, and that asymmetry is not
  conventional. So there is no "right in its own language" symmetry to deflate to.

This is the first priority claim in the investigation to clear I5/I6. The first run could not
decide bead/knot because the rivals were isomorphic; Q-016 *can* decide operation/arrow because a
witness (`FinSet`) structurally separates them and the generative asymmetry is real.

## 4. Verdict and the rescue

**Distinction is weakly prior to the arrow.** The operation furnishes the space of forms; the
arrow is the least/greatest selection on it; the arrow presupposes the space, the space does not
presuppose the arrow; and the priority is supra-structural and decidable, not an I6 bedrock.

**What it rescues, stated exactly.** The program's load-bearing claim died, in the first run, as
an *ontological* claim about the world's joints (bead/knot, sub-structural, undecidable). It
**revives at the level of the primitives**: not "things are residues of relations," but
**"distinction is prior to the arrow"** — the operation opens the space of forms that time /
orientation / the arrow presupposes and orders. The knot picture, relocated to this register, is
**true**, and demonstrably so (the `FinSet` witness). The first place the whole program lands a
priority claim on the positive side of the ledger.

## 5. Bounds (so the rescue is not oversold)

- **Weak, not strong.** Distinction is prior to *the arrow*, not to everything. The genesis still
  needs *both* (Q-015: numerals and the All require the arrow's two poles). "Weakly prior" =
  furnishes-the-ground-the-other-presupposes, not produces.
- **The *operation* is what is prior — not yet "distinction" in the essays' sense.** The thing
  shown weakly prior is the self-differentiating operation (functor + self-application). Its
  identification with the essays' "distinction"/"the between" is **Q-001 / Q-017**, still open.
- **Paradigm-relative.** `FinSet`-vs-`Set` is a *within*-paradigm witness. The cross-paradigm
  question (**Q-013**) stands: a non-(co)algebraic account could in principle order the dependence
  differently. The witness is clean *inside* the fixed-point paradigm.
- **Residue resolved, not recurred.** The usual I6 individuation residue does **not** bite here,
  because §3 shows the descriptions are not inter-translatable. This is the exception, and it is
  what makes Q-016 a genuine positive.

## 6. What this changes

- **Promote the result to a Position (`P`).** "Distinction (the self-differentiating operation) is
  weakly prior to the arrow (time/orientation), supra-structurally and within the fixed-point
  paradigm; witnessed by `FinSet`." First `P` the program can write — it has an argument and a
  decisive case (the witness) and survives the I6 deflation.
- **Q-002 (strong vs co-primitive) closes toward weak-priority**, not co-primitivity: the
  asymmetry survives, relocated from relata/relation to operation/arrow.
- **Q-001 / Q-017 become the live frontier:** is the operation that is weakly prior identical to
  Zero-the-field / the Between? If yes, the essays' own primitive is what the rescue vindicates.
- **Q-013 (cross-paradigm) is the remaining threat** to generality, now the single most important
  open question: the rescue holds in the paradigm; does it hold outside it?
