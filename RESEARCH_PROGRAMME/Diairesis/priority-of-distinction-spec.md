# The Priority of Distinction

*A Metaphysical Research Program — Spec*
*Status: draft. Built on the metaphysical-spec format (single document, no empirical
floor). Scope: determine whether ontological priority runs from things to the
distinctions among them or the other way, and identify which sub-questions are
settleable as metaphysics and which are bedrock.*

> This spec is **free-standing**. It does not take any particular text as its
> subject; the genesis-of-distinction essays (Diairesis/Between) become one *source
> of candidate answers* alongside structural realism, category-theoretic
> foundations, and the no-self tradition, not the thing under study. The question is
> posed so that anyone could ask it cold.

---

## §1 — Problem Statement

The motivating question is whether what is fundamental is a stock of self-standing
*things* that then stand in relations and distinctions, or a fabric of *distinctions
and relations* of which "things" are only the residue — whether determinate being
arrives in units that are subsequently related, the way beads are threaded on a
string and each bead keeps its identity off the string, or in cuts that deposit
their own terms, the way a knot is nothing but a configuration of the cord, owns no
material of its own, and is not there before the cord is tied. Call these the
**bead picture** (terms first, relations external and added) and the **knot
picture** (distinctions first, terms constituted and left behind).

The question is poorly posed as stated, because "what is fundamental" and "prior"
each name several things at once. It is asked at three different structural **levels**
— the term, the relation/distinction, and the act that institutes the difference
between them — and with three different **senses of priority** — constructional,
dependence, and absolute — and the answer differs by cell. Most disputes in this
area are two parties each asserting something true in a different cell and mistaking
the difference for disagreement: a structural realist denying intrinsic particles
(term level, dependence sense) talks past a logician building numbers from the empty
set (term level, *constructional* sense), and both talk past whoever is asking after
the unconditioned ground (act level, absolute sense). The first job of any program
here is to replace the monolithic "relations versus relata" with the layered
version.

Once that is done, the live question reduces to something sharp. At the *dependence*
sense, on the *relation* level: is the term-level **closed under distinction** — is
every determinate term the residue of some cut, with no term standing on its own — or
is it **open**, admitting individuals that no distinction produces? And is that
closure-status **uniform** across domains (logic, mathematics, physics, mind) or
**variable** — knot-like in physics, say, but bead-like in logic? Closure-and-uniform
is the knot picture vindicated; openness, or domain-variability, breaks it. This is
the metaphysical analog of asking for the intrinsic dimensionality of a space and
whether it is fixed: sharp, forkable, consequential — and, honestly, *arguable rather
than estimable*, since unlike a dimensionality there is no measurement that returns a
value the disputants did not choose. The tractability-boundary section later in the
spec is where that difference is paid for.

What it bears on: **(i)** adjudication between substance metaphysics and structuralism
(ontic structural realism, category-theoretic foundations) on a structured rather than
rhetorical basis — which cell each view actually occupies, and whether they conflict
or merely differ in level; **(ii)** the foundations of mathematics: whether number is
built from units (set-theoretic, with the axiom of foundation guarding against
self-membership) or from operations and morphisms (category-theoretic, objects as
identities), and whether a self-inclusive totality is coherent; **(iii)** the
metaphysics of self: whether there is a subject-thing at the center or only a process,
and what each predicts about the first-person case; **(iv)** the boundary between what
metaphysics can settle and what is constitutively undecidable or merely verbal — the
analog, for this question, of the line where an empirical program meets the hard
problem.

## §2 — Conceptual Scaffolding

No claim in this program is admissible until it has been assigned (a) to a **level**
and (b) to a **sense of priority**. Most confusion in the existing literature comes
from claims that silently switch cells mid-argument.

### 2.1 The three levels

| Level | What it is | Bead/knot analogy | Plausible character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Term** | The candidate self-standing items: particulars, units, points, simples, "ones" | The bead — has identity off the string | Contested; in fundamental physics (OSR) it leans residual, but nowhere settled |
| **Relation / distinction** | The cuts, differences, relations, betweens — the structure | The knotting — the configuration that holds | Where the knot picture is *strongest and most defensible*; the live layer |
| **Act / ground** | Not an item in the field and not a relation in it, but the condition for there being a field of terms-and-relations at all — the drawing of the first distinction, or the undifferentiated from which it is drawn | The tying, and the hands — not a bead, not a knot | A category sits here, not a content; treating it as a term *or* a relation is the standard error |

The levels are not three regions of one space; the third is a condition on there
being a space. The signature mistake — the analog of treating an altered self/world
frame as just another emotion — is to promote the **act/ground** to a super-term (a
biggest individual: the One, the All-as-object, a first thing) or demote it to a
super-relation (a relation with no terms). It is neither. The question "is the ground
a thing or a relation?" is, at this level, probably ill-formed, and recognizing that
is the cheapest result the layering yields.

**The answer differs by level**, which is what earns the layering: at the *term*
level, whether anything is genuinely self-standing is an open and possibly bedrock
question; at the *relation* level, the dependence of terms on structure is real,
defensible, and the program's center of gravity; at the *act/ground* level, the
priority question as posed is a category error and converts into a different question
(what institutes the term/relation distinction — and whether *that* has a
determinate answer).

### 2.2 The three senses of priority

1. **Constructional priority** — X is prior to Y if your theory builds Y out of X.
   Cheap and theory-relative: numbers from sets *or* sets from numbers, depending on
   the axioms. Trivially settles either way and proves nothing about the world.
   *Should be excluded from priority claims to avoid inflating results*, exactly as
   combinatorial novelty is excluded in the affective case.
2. **Dependence priority** — X is prior to Y if Y depends on X for its existence or
   identity and not conversely (grounding, ontological dependence). The live sense.
   The real fork — does the relation merely *organize* terms already there, or
   *constitute* them? — lives here. This is the carving-style crux: the analog of
   "label versus constitute."
3. **Absolute priority** — X is the unconditioned, with nothing prior to it and
   nothing "behind" it. The hard case. Partly entangled with whether "absolutely
   first" even has a truth-condition, or is like asking what lies north of the North
   Pole — which routes it toward the deflation/aporia machinery rather than toward a
   verdict.

### 2.3 The assignment rule

Every priority claim is tagged ⟨level, sense⟩ before admission. An untagged claim is
not yet a claim; it is a question about which cell it occupies. Worked tags:

- "Numbers are constructed from the empty set and succession" → ⟨term/relation,
  **constructional**⟩. Trivial, theory-relative; excluded from priority verdicts.
- "Particles are just nodes in the structure of physical relations" (OSR) → ⟨term,
  **dependence**⟩. A live, carving-style claim — the strongest home of the knot
  picture.
- "A sign's identity is purely differential" (Saussurean value) → ⟨relation,
  **dependence**⟩, *restricted to the semiotic domain* — a candidate case of
  domain-variable closure (knot-like here; says nothing about physics).
- "The act of distinction is the unconditioned ground" → ⟨act, **absolute**⟩. The hard
  cell; routes to the tractability boundary, not to a discriminating case.
- "There is no self, only the process" → ⟨term, **dependence**⟩ at the level of the
  subject — the knot picture applied to mind; its truth at this cell is logically
  independent of its truth at the physical cell.

### 2.4 What the scaffolding has already bought

Before a single crux is run, the layering has done three things. It has shown that
the headline "relations versus relata" is at least nine questions (three levels ×
three senses), most of them not in dispute. It has quarantined constructional
priority, which otherwise generates fake wins for either picture. And it has located
the one cell the rest of the spec will live in — ⟨relation, dependence⟩, the closure
question — while flagging the cell that will resist it — ⟨act, absolute⟩, where the
question is likely either verbal or bedrock. The cruxes, the discriminating-case
table, and the intractability triage follow in the next pass.
