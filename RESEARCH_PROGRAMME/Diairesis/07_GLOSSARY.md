# Glossary

> Project-internal definitions, one paragraph each, in this programme's sense. Each
> notes its upstream source and where it diverges; an undivergent term is flagged as
> such. The point of the divergence line is to stop the programme from quietly
> meaning something idiosyncratic by a borrowed word.

## Act of distinction

The primitive of the programme: the taking of something to be something rather than
nothing. It is not an operation on pre-existing terms; it *produces* its terms. A
single act yields two co-original poles at once (One and Zero, or marked and
unmarked). **Upstream:** Spencer-Brown's "draw a distinction" (*Laws of Form* 1969).
**Divergence:** Spencer-Brown takes the injunction and its asymmetry as bedrock; this
programme asks whether the asymmetry can be derived ([Q-004](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-004))
and whether the act is itself a One ([Q-003](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-003)).

## Zero (field) vs. zero (numeral)

Two strictly separated senses. **Zero-as-field** is the undifferentiated background,
co-original with One, ontologically prior to no determination because it *is* the
absence of determination; it is "full" only in the sense that pure indeterminacy
admits no internal gaps. **zero-as-numeral** is the reified absence-of-distinction
entered into the number series *after* counting is under way; it is logically
posterior to One. **Upstream:** Hegel's Nothing (*Wissenschaft der Logik*); Badiou's
void. **Divergence:** the two-level guard (field vs. numeral) is sharper than Hegel's
Being↔Nothing passage and is load-bearing for the programme; conflating the two is the
error the distinction exists to prevent.

## One (dual character)

Not a primitive datum but the product of the act, with two aspects of a single event:
*compression* (a virtual many treated, for now, as a single entity — what happens
*within* the boundary) and *discrete unit* (a single term against an indeterminate
ground — what happens *across* the boundary). **Upstream:** the count-as-one
(Badiou); the unit of arithmetic. **Divergence:** the insistence that these are two
descriptions of one act, not two entities, and that the unity is unstable because the
suppressed multiplicity can reassert itself.

## Singular One vs. categorical One

The two kinds of unity whose joint count produces Two. The **singular One** is the
bounded entity, the discrete product of the first act. The **categorical One** is the
whole field of potential multiplicity taken as a single class. They differ in kind (a
bounded object vs. an open-ended totality), and recognising both as elements of one
framework is the second-order act that generates Two. **Upstream:** none direct; the
type/token and element/class distinctions are cousins. **Divergence:** original to the
programme; the genesis of Two from *two kinds of One* (not from Zero and One, as in von
Neumann) is the specific claim (essay 1 §III).

## Fractionation / aggregation

The two-way process that One *is*. **Fractionation** is the descending, analytic
direction: any One can be re-opened into constituent ones. **Aggregation** is the
ascending, synthetic direction: any collection of ones can be gathered into a new,
more encompassing One. One is the name for the oscillation between them, not a static
point. **Upstream:** analysis/synthesis (general); part/whole (mereology).
**Divergence:** the claim that these are not two operations on numbers but the *being*
of One; and the open question of whether the "oscillation" is temporal
([Q-007](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-007)).

## The All (one of ones)

The single totality that any count produces: a One composed of Ones that contains
Zero (reified as numeral), the Many, and *itself* (since the All, as a unity, is a
One). Its self-inclusion is the seed of Russell's paradox. **Upstream:** Cantor's
inconsistent multiplicity; the Hegelian Absolute. **Divergence:** the programme does
not resolve the self-inclusion by fiat (no axiom of foundation); it tracks it as an
open structural question ([Q-009](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-009)) and treats the
All as a pressure the system produces, possibly cannot consummate.

## The Between (the interval)

The second essay's primitive: the gap the cut opens, prior to its sides. "The cut is
not a cut *in* anything; the cut is the first thing, and its sides are deposited by
it." The marked and unmarked are residue the between leaves at its ends. **Upstream:**
relations-prior-to-relata (OSR); Spencer-Brown's distinction; arguably Derrida's
*différance* as spacing and Heidegger's *Zwischen*. **Divergence:** the strict
priority claim (the between is prior, not co-primitive,
[Q-002](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-002)); and the open question of whether the
Between is the same structure as Zero-as-field ([Q-001](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-001)).

## Relatum-as-residue

The thesis that a term (a point, an instant, a node) is constituted entirely by its
relations and is nothing else — "the residue the relation leaves at its ends." Holds
strictly at the minimal case (contentless terms); the essay grants that stones, having
content, are not residue in this sense, but locates the metaphysical claim at the
bottom, where terms have no content. **Upstream:** structural realism; the bundle
theory of objects. **Divergence:** the *asymmetry* — terms are *thinner* than
relations — against the symmetric co-primitive reading the programme refuses.

## The vortex (thing as turning around a void)

The model object for relatum-as-residue: a vortex owns no water, is empty at the
centre, and is made entirely of the medium's between-states (differential velocities,
pressure gradients). The eye — the central void — is the thing's principle, not its
defect: fill the centre and the vortex dissolves into undifferentiated flow.
Generalised: every thing is a vortex, and the self is the turning around an empty eye.
**Upstream:** process philosophy (Whitehead); the wave as "a shape the water is
doing." **Divergence:** the load-bearing use of the *constitutive void*, and the
exposure to the medium objection ([Q-010](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-010)) — a
vortex needs water, and the programme owes an account of the "water" that is not a
smuggled substrate.

## Threshold

The criterion that separates signal from noise, One from Zero, in the computational
instance. Itself an act of distinction, requiring prior calibration ("enough"). The
programme's candidate site for the claim that the binary is always the quantised
output of a prior *continuous* process — that distinction has a suppressed metric
aspect. **Upstream:** logic gates; signal detection. **Divergence:** the reading of
the threshold *as* the between, giving distinction a metric/degree structure the
binary outcome hides ([Q-008](02_OPEN_QUESTIONS_REGISTRY.md#q-008)).

## Position (P) / Construction (C) / Probe (S)

The programme's three artefact types, re-cut from the formal sister programme's
theorem/conjecture/study. A **Position** is a claim the programme owns, with an
argument and a stated decisive case that would break it. A **Construction** is an
attempt to formalise a core claim (in category theory, Laws of Form, set theory,
mereology), settled positively if it goes through without smuggling and negatively if
it must smuggle. A **Probe** is a cross-check against an instance (computation,
physics, phenomenology), which is evidence and never settlement. **Upstream:** the
Isonomia/Mesh programme's T/C/S apparatus. **Divergence:** the re-cut for metaphysics,
and the added **aporetic** category for questions that are constitutively unsettleable
from inside (see [`00_CHARTER.md`](00_CHARTER.md) §0b).
