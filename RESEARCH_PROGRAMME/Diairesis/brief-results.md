**Q.** Does priority run things→distinctions (bead) or distinctions→things (knot)?

## 1. First run (ontological framing)
- Both pictures coherent. Empirically equivalent (no observation separates).
- Difference is **sub-structural** (I5): bead-world / knot-world isomorphic in all structure → below the isomorphism ceiling → no formal/physical method indexes it.

- **Verdict:** no winner; undecidable as posed.

## 2. Reframe (excavation)
- Ontological Q (undecidable) → constructive Q: *what is the minimal self-differentiating operation, and what does it generate under iteration?*
- Gain: pass/fail. Targets = scaffolding's own apparatus: (T2) act/relation/term levels; (T3) marked/unmarked asymmetry; (T1) numerals.

## 4. Coalgebra test — does *any* self-differentiating operation generate the arrow?
- **A (dynamics).** Irreversible dynamics ⟺ embedded `(ℕ, succ)` (Prop.). Operation arrow-neutral (swap/constant coalgebras cycle; canonical shift does both). `productivity ⊥ irreversibility`. → arrow not in dynamics; localized to carrier.
- **B (carrier construction).** All routes import the arrow: Adámek terminal sequence (ω-index, converges at ω for streams); metric/Banach (depth-grading = ℕ + posited completeness); dcpo (directed-completeness axiom); guarded recursion (`▷`-clock; semantics = presheaves over (ℕ,≤)). **Conservation:** arrow in carrier ⟺ ordinal in construction. → not generated by construction.
- **C (initial-algebra end).** Distinction-functor `1 + X` is arrow-neutral (cyclic algebras `ℤ/n` are legal). `ℕ` selected by **initial = least = well-founded**. `μ` (least) / `ν` (greatest) = the two directions of the arrow. Three escape routes all smuggle: construct (spend ω-colimit) / posit (NNO axiom; FinSet = topos w/o NNO) / encode (System F impredicativity borrows a larger ordinal). → not generated at initial-algebra end.
- **Verdict:** FAIL in full. Arrow = genuine **second irreducible primitive** across the fixed-point paradigm. `{operation, arrow}` does **not** collapse.

## Established
- Distinction **generates**: the relational/level skeleton + the *space* of fixed-point forms (cyclic and well-founded alike). Non-smuggled.
- Distinction does **not** generate: the **arrow** (irreversibility / orientation / order). The arrow *is* the least/greatest (μ/ν, induction/coinduction) selection imposed on that space.
- Master Q, partial answer: distinction **prior to the skeleton**, **co-primitive with the arrow** (time). (Was wholly I6; now partly determinate.)
- **Q-007 (genesis of time) terminates with a reason:** time = the selection principle the calculus of distinction presupposes to pick determinate forms from the space it opens.

## Bound
- Result holds **relative to the fixed-point / (co)algebra paradigm** — the only paradigm in which "self-differentiation" is precise. Not proven tout court.

## Open residue
- A non-fixed-point account of self-differentiation could in principle generate the arrow (untested). Every candidate named (re-entry, coalgebra, autopoiesis-as-fixed-point) lives in this paradigm; failure-reason is structural and uniform (μ/ν = arrow) → robust where tested.
- Scope §5: is there an order/clock whose directedness is forced internally, not by an ℕ-semantics? (guarded recursion suggests no; not proven).

## Primitive base (final)
**{ self-differentiating operation, arrow }** — two irreducibles, neither reducible to the other within the paradigm; the second (time) located and explained, not eliminated.
