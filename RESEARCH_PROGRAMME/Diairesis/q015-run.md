## 4. The two-way process ‚Äî One itself

Essay 1 ¬ßIV: "One is the name for the two-way process: **aggregation** (ascending, synthetic)
and **fractionation** (descending, analytic)." These are exactly the two directions of the
fixed-point duality:

- **Aggregation** = gather many into a unit = **fold / algebra**: `F(X) ‚Üí X`. The `Œº`-direction.
- **Fractionation** = analyse a unit into its constituents = **unfold / coalgebra**: `X ‚Üí F(X)`.
  The `ŒΩ`-direction.

So **One = the fold/unfold (`Œº`/`ŒΩ`) duality applied to the self-differentiating operation.**
Essay 1's "two-way process that One *is*" is the algebra/coalgebra duality, exactly. The genesis
closes ‚Äî over the single operation `1 + X`, with both poles of the arrow.

## 5. The finding: the arrow is bidirectional

The coalgebra test established "the arrow" but exhibited it only at its `Œº`-pole ‚Äî irreversible
accumulation, a first, the numerals (Check A's embedded `(‚Ñï, succ)`; Check C's `initial = least`).
Q-015 shows the genesis equally requires the `ŒΩ`-pole ‚Äî the non-well-founded, self-inclusive,
coinductive direction (the All, `‚àû = succ ‚àû`). And the two poles are **dual but independent**:

- `Œº` and `ŒΩ` are categorically dual (initial algebra / final coalgebra; colimit of the initial
  sequence / limit of the terminal sequence).
- Neither constructs the other: `ŒΩ ‚äã Œº` (the conatural `‚àû` is in `ŒΩ`, not `Œº`), and a setting can
  have one without the other (different universal constructions, different end-objects `0`/`1`).

So the second primitive is not "irreversible accumulation" (one direction) but **bidirectional
directedness** ‚Äî least *and* greatest, fold *and* unfold, aggregation *and* fractionation. The
coalgebra test undercounted it because the numerals (its probe) live entirely at the `Œº`-pole.

**No fourth ingredient.** Levels and duality (operation), orientation and numerals and aggregation
(`Œº`), the All and fractionation (`ŒΩ`) ‚Äî everything in the genesis generates from `{operation,
bidirectional arrow}`. Nothing forced a genuinely new primitive.

## 6. Verdict

**PASS, conditional on the refinement.** The full genesis generates from the two-primitive base ‚Äî
*provided* "arrow" is read as bidirectional directedness rather than one-directional accumulation.
The refinement is not a patch; it is a result: the genesis itself forces the arrow to be two-poled,
and the two poles reproduce essay 1's own aggregation/fractionation structure. Concretely, the
entire apparatus lives over one functor `1 + X`, with `Œº = ‚Ñï` (the Many) and `ŒΩ = ‚ÑïÃÑ` (the Many +
the self-inclusive All) as its two fixed points.

**Residue (I6-flavoured, recorded not resolved).** Is the bidirectional arrow **one** primitive
(directedness, manifesting as both poles ‚Äî e.g. a complete-lattice / order structure that yields
both `Œº` and `ŒΩ` by Knaster‚ÄìTarski) or **two** (the initial-object-`0`/`‚ä•` posit and the
terminal-object-`1`/`‚ä§` posit are dual but separate existence assumptions)? `Œº`-completeness
(`‚ä•` + ascending sups) does not by itself give `ŒΩ`-completeness (`‚ä§` + descending infs); the full
bidirectional arrow is the *complete* structure, both ends. Whether "two dual ends" is one thing
or two is the same individuation question (I6) that recurs throughout ‚Äî so the primitive base is
`{operation, arrow}` with the arrow's internal count (one bipolar / two polar) left at the
standing bedrock.

**Feed-forward to Q-016 (is distinction weakly prior?).** This run sharpens the positive horn.
The operation furnishes the **space** ‚Äî the algebras and coalgebras of `1 + X`, the lattice of
forms. Both arrow-poles (`Œº`, `ŒΩ`) *operate on* that space: they *select* within it (least,
greatest) but do not *furnish* it. The space exists (with its cyclic, un-oriented forms) before
either pole selects. So distinction (the operation) plausibly remains **weakly prior**: it
provides what both poles of the arrow act on, while neither pole provides the space. Q-016's
positive horn ‚Äî distinction prior because it furnishes the space the arrow orients ‚Äî is now
concretely in view, and is the natural next run.

**Net.** Genesis closes. Two primitives hold (no third ingredient). The arrow is bidirectional
(the coalgebra test caught one pole; the All needs the other). The whole genesis sits over `1 + X`
