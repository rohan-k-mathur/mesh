# C005 — The time-indexed family `{B_t}` of substrate behaviours under deliberative-move application forms a directed system whose colimit characterises convergence

- **status:** open
- **ring:** core
- **depends-on:** C004
- **linked-open-questions:** Q-005, Q-009
- **last-reviewed:** 2026-05-27

## Statement

Equip the time-indexed family `{B_t}_{t ∈ T}` of substrate behaviours
generated by a single deliberation's update history with the partial
transition maps `φ_{t,t+1}: B_t ⇀ B_{t+1}` induced by `σ_joint` applied to
the witness-record set at step `t+1`. Then:

1. `(T, ≤)` is directed.
2. The family `(B_t, φ)` is a directed system in the category of behaviours
   (with partial behaviour morphisms as arrows).
3. The colimit `colim_T B_t` exists and is canonically isomorphic to the
   "convergent-design subset" of the limit behaviour: the set of designs
   `D` such that for some `t`, `D ∈ B_t` and `D` participates in a
   daimon-terminating interaction with every counter-design in `B_t^⊥`
   that is `walked` or `witnessable` at `t`.

## Positive settlement

A construction of the colimit plus a verification of the iso. The proof
must explicitly handle the case where two updates produce *incompatible*
behaviours (e.g. an additive choice that lights up after the substrate's
v2 cross-cone work).

## Negative settlement

A two-step deliberation where the family fails to be directed: updates at
`t = 1` and `t = 2` produce `B_1` and `B_2` with no common `B_3` extending
both, despite both being reachable from `B_0`.
