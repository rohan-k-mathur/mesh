# AI Authoring Policy

> Track AI-EPI Pt. 3 ¬ß5

This document is the load-bearing trust statement that accompanies the
`POST /api/v3/arguments/ai-draft` surface. It explains *what* AI-authored
arguments mean on Isonomia, *what they don't*, and the structural
guarantees the platform makes about them.

## What an AI-authored argument is

An argument whose `authorKind` is `AI` (or `HYBRID`, for human-edited AI
drafts) has been generated by a language model from an editor-supplied
prompt + sources, then persisted via the `aiAuthoring.createAiDraft`
flow. Every AI-authored row carries an `aiProvenance` JSON blob with:

- `model` ‚Äî the display name of the model that produced the draft
- `promptHash` ‚Äî a stable hash of the prompt + sources, for reproducibility
- `sourceUrls` ‚Äî the external sources the draft was asked to cite
- `generatedAt` ‚Äî the timestamp of the draft
- `hint` ‚Äî the editor's free-text hint, if provided
- `schemeKey` ‚Äî the ASPIC scheme the draft was targeted at

This metadata is surfaced in every public attestation envelope (`author.kind`
+ `author.aiProvenance`), so any downstream consumer ‚Äî including other
LLMs ‚Äî can tell that a given argument was AI-drafted.

## What it isn't

AI-authored arguments are **not**:

1. A claim that the platform endorses the argument.
2. A substitute for human deliberative engagement.
3. A way to inflate dialectical fitness or standing scores.

## Structural guarantees

Two invariants are enforced in code, not just policy.

### Invariant 1 ‚Äî No auto-publishing

`createAiDraft` does **not** mint an `ArgumentPermalink`. Drafts are
invisible to the public retrieval surfaces (search, MCP, attestation
endpoint) until an editor explicitly approves them via
`POST /api/v3/arguments/ai-draft/[id]/approve`.

### Invariant 2 ‚Äî Always enter at `untested-default`

The standing-state classifier (`computeStandingState` in
`lib/citations/argumentAttestation.ts`) reads from *inbound dialectical
traffic only* ‚Äî answered CQs, attack rows, support rows. It does not
read from authoring metadata. As a result, an AI-authored row at the
moment of approval has zero answered CQs, zero attacks, zero supports,
and therefore lands in `untested-default`.

The standing label remains honest as the row gathers engagement: a
sufficiently scrutinised AI-authored row will eventually be marked
`tested-survived`, but only after distinct human challengers have
recorded structural objections that the row has answered. Standing
depth (`StandingDepth.confidence`) reports this density honestly so a
sophisticated reader can distinguish "tested-survived (1 challenger,
0 reviewers, thin)" from "tested-survived (8 challengers, 12 reviewers,
dense)".

## Counter-citation reflex

The `cite_argument` MCP tool already attaches the strongest known
counter-argument to every citation. AI-authored arguments are *not*
exempt from this ‚Äî citing one always surfaces its dialectical
opposition.
